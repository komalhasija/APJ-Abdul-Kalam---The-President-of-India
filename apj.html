A.P.J.Abdul Kalam
1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Avul pakir Jainnulabdeen Abdul Kalam (/'a:bdel ke'la::m/(about the soundlisten);15 October 1931-27 July 2015) was an indian aerospace scientist who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameshwaram,Tamil Nadu and studied Physics and aerospace engineering . He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator,mainly at a Defence Research and Developnment Organisation (DSRO) and Indian Space Research Institute (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile developnment efforts. He thus came to the known as the Missile Man ofIndia for his work on the developnment of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology [2][3][4]He also played a pivotial organisational,technical,and politicalrole in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998,the first since the original nuclear test by india in 1974
